================================================================================
                    PHONG LIGHTING FIX - QUICK SUMMARY
================================================================================

PROBLEM:
  Phong animation was showing brown/orange gradient with black areas instead
  of a properly lit 3D sphere with specular highlights.

ROOT CAUSES:
  1. Missing GL_SPECULAR light component
  2. Fragment shader not handling uninitialized material properties
  3. GL_COLOR_MATERIAL not enabled for proper material pipeline
  4. Shader not receiving fallback color information

================================================================================
                              FILES MODIFIED
================================================================================

1. Aux_3D.py
   - Added: LIGHT_SPECULAR = [1.0, 1.0, 1.0, 1.0]
   - Enhanced init_opengl() with:
     * glLightfv(GL_LIGHT0, GL_SPECULAR, LIGHT_SPECULAR)
     * glEnable(GL_COLOR_MATERIAL)
     * glColorMaterial(GL_FRONT, GL_AMBIENT_AND_DIFFUSE)

2. phong_vertex.glsl
   - Added: varying vec4 vertex_color
   - Pass vertex color to fragment shader for fallback material info

3. phong_fragment.glsl
   - Added intelligent fallback checks for:
     * Material ambient (fallback to vertex_color * 0.3)
     * Material diffuse (fallback to vertex_color)
     * Shininess (fallback to 32.0)
     * Specular (fallback to white vec3(1.0))
   - Complete Phong model: I = ambient + diffuse + specular

4. LightingPhong.py
   - Enhanced enable() to ensure GL_LIGHTING and GL_LIGHT0 are active
